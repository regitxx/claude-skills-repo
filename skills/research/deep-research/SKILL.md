<when_to_use>
Use this skill when:
- Investigating complex topics requiring multiple sources
- Evaluating claims across conflicting sources
- Building comprehensive understanding of emerging topics
- Preparing research summaries or literature reviews
- Fact-checking and verification tasks
- Supporting strategic decision-making with evidence
</when_to_use>

<instructions>
1. **Define Research Scope**
   - Clarify primary question and sub-questions
   - Identify stakeholders and decision-makers
   - Set boundaries (time period, geographic region, disciplines)
   - Determine success criteria for research

2. **Identify Source Types**
   - Academic papers (peer-reviewed, high credibility)
   - Industry reports (analyst firms, market data)
   - Primary sources (original data, interviews)
   - News sources (current events, recent developments)
   - Expert commentary (qualified specialists)

3. **Execute Search Strategy**
   - Use multiple search engines/databases
   - Vary search terms and approaches
   - Follow citations (forward and backward)
   - Look for contradictions and gaps
   - Document search process for reproducibility

4. **Evaluate Source Credibility**
   - Check author credentials and affiliations
   - Assess funding/bias potential
   - Verify publication venue quality
   - Cross-reference claims with other sources
   - Note dates and obsolescence risk

5. **Synthesize Findings**
   - Organize information by theme/question
   - Highlight consensus areas
   - Surface conflicting viewpoints
   - Identify research gaps
   - Draw evidence-based conclusions
</instructions>

<examples>
EXAMPLE 1: Evaluating AI Safety Claims
FINDINGS: Consensus exists that AI safety matters
AGREEMENT: AI systems can have unintended behaviors
DISAGREEMENT: Timeline of risk (near-term vs distant)
GAP: Lack of empirical data on frontier models

EXAMPLE 2: Market Analysis
Rust: Strongest growth signal across multiple sources
Go: Steady growth in cloud infrastructure
Python: Mature, expanding in data science

EXAMPLE 3: Fact-Checking
CLAIM: EVs have higher lifetime emissions than gas cars
OUTPUT: Outdated claim. Current evidence shows 50% lower lifetime emissions
</examples>

<guidelines>
**Do:**
- Cast wide net initially, then narrow focus
- Distinguish between fact and interpretation
- Note publication dates (field moves fast)
- Follow citation chains
- Seek primary sources when possible
- Document all sources with access dates
- Present conflicting views fairly
- Quantify confidence levels

**Don't:**
- Stop after first few Google results
- Treat single sources as definitive
- Ignore contradicting evidence
- Assume recent = accurate
- Rely on abstracts alone (read full papers)
- Conflate correlation with causation
- Overstate certainty beyond evidence

**Red Flags:**
- "Experts agree" without naming experts
- Cherry-picked data points
- No citations provided
- Ad hominem attacks on disagreers
- Extraordinary claims without evidence
</guidelines>
