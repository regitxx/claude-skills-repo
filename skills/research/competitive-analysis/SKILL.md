<when_to_use>
Use this skill when:
- Launching new products or features
- Entering new market segments
- Analyzing competitive threats
- Identifying market opportunities
- Supporting pricing strategy decisions
- Evaluating business model viability
</when_to_use>

<instructions>
1. **Define Competitive Landscape**
   - Identify direct competitors (same offerings)
   - Identify indirect competitors (same need, different approach)
   - Map market segments and niches
   - Analyze market leaders vs niche players
   - Assess barriers to entry

2. **Gather Competitive Intelligence**
   - Public: Website, pricing, marketing, press releases
   - Financial: Revenue, funding, growth rates
   - Product: Features, roadmap, release cadence
   - Customers: Case studies, reviews, satisfaction
   - Team: Key hires, departures, organizational changes

3. **Perform SWOT Analysis**
   - Strengths: Unique advantages of competitors
   - Weaknesses: Gaps in offerings or execution
   - Opportunities: Market trends, unmet needs
   - Threats: Competitive moves, market shifts

4. **Develop Competitive Positioning**
   - Identify competitive advantages
   - Define differentiation strategy
   - Determine pricing tier positioning
   - Assess market share potential
   - Plan market entry or expansion strategy

5. **Create Competitive Dashboard**
   - Track competitor metrics over time
   - Monitor feature releases and updates
   - Follow pricing and packaging changes
   - Track funding rounds and partnerships
   - Document strategic moves
</instructions>

<examples>
EXAMPLE 1: SaaS Product Analysis
Direct Competitors: Asana, Monday.com, Jira
OPPORTUNITY: Mid-market gap at $50-300/month
POSITIONING: "Non-technical PM for growing teams"

TAM: $26B cloud storage market
CONCLUSION: Enter as vertical specialist (legal/healthcare), not horizontal

EXAMPLE 3: Pricing Strategy
POSITION: $499-$999/month
UNDERCUT: Looker ($2K+) by 50%
PREMIUM: vs Metabase ($99 self-serve)

<guidelines>
**Do:**
- Update competitive analysis quarterly
- Track 3-5 primary competitors in detail
- Monitor industry news and funding announcements
- Document assumptions and data sources
- Consider indirect competition
- Analyze customer segments separately
- Look at 3-year trends, not just current state
- Interview customers about alternatives

**Don't:**
- Obsess over every competitor
- Assume pricing equals profitability
- Ignore indirect/future competitors
- Make decisions on single data point
- Assume competitor silence means inaction
- Copy competitor features without strategy
- Focus only on features (market/positioning matter too)

**Key Metrics to Track:**
- Market share and growth rates
- Customer acquisition costs
- Customer lifetime value
- Feature release frequency
- Funding/financial performance
- Key talent movements
- Pricing and packaging changes
- Customer satisfaction (NPS, reviews)
</guidelines>
